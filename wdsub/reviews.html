BOOK REVIEW

The Hobbit

"The Hobbit" by J.R.R. Tolkien is a classic fantasy adventure story about Bilbo Baggins, a home-loving hobbit who is unexpectedly drawn into a quest with a wizard and a group of dwarves to reclaim their treasure from a fearsome dragon, Smaug.

Animal Farm

"Animal Farm" by George Orwell is a satirical allegory that uses a story about rebellious farm animals to critique the dangers of totalitarianism and power corruption, specifically drawing parallels to the Russian Revolution through its characters like Napoleon (Stalin) and Snowball (Trotsky)

Narrative of the Life of Fredrick Douglass

"Narrative of the Life of Frederick Douglass, an American Slave" is a powerful autobiography detailing the early life of a formerly enslaved man, Frederick Douglass, where he vividly recounts the brutal realities of slavery in Maryland
